Ideal Speaker Placement for Home Theater

On this page I will briefly cover some of the most common speaker layouts for surround sound and Dolby Atmos/dtsX.

5.1 Channel Surround

This is the Dolby specification layout for a five speaker surround setup. The rear surrounds can either be directly to the sides or slightly behind the main listening position.

7.1 Channel Surround

This is the layout for a seven speaker surround, where there are speakers both to your sides and behind you. This provides a more immersive effect, as objects can move from front to back.

5.1.2 Dolby Atmos

This is the layout for a five speaker Dolby Atmos system, with the addition of height speakers in the ceiling. You can also achieve this by mounting speakers high on the wall or by reflecting the sound off the ceiling.

7.1.4 Dolby Atmos

This is the same as 7.1, but with four added height channels. This is the preferred Dolby Atmos configuration because four height speakers allow the sounds to move not only above you, but behind or in front above you. Most higher end recievers support a 7.1.4 setup, at least for processing.
