Terms and Definitions

Channel
All the sounds coming from one individual speaker in your system.
Dolby Atmos
3D spatial audio surround sound that uses object based audio instead of channel based.
2.1, 5.1, 7.1, 5.1.2, 7.1.4
The first number represents the number of ear level or "bedlayer" speakers. The second number represents the number of subwoofer channels. If there is a third number, it represents the number of height or "Atmos" speakers in a system.
Audio/video (AV) reciever
A piece of equipment that takes inncoming audio signals from a TV or other device, decodes and processes them, amplifies the signal, and then sends it out to your speakers, or first to an amplifier, and then speakers. It also passes video signals through to your TV if coming from another device.
Audio Return Channel and Enhanced Audio Return Channel (ARC/eARC)
Sends audio signal from your TV to your receiver, which then processes and amplifies the sound.
